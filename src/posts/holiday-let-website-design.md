## Why Are OTA Commission Rates a Bigger Problem Than Ever in 2026?

Airbnb's host fee rose to 15.5% across the board when the new structure fully rolled out in April 2026, and Booking.com sits at 15–18% as standard ([Houst](https://houst.com/), 2026). On a property generating £30,000 a year, that's between £4,650 and £5,400 walking out the door before you've paid for a single changeover clean.

For a few years, many hosts accepted this as the cost of visibility. But the maths has shifted. Commission creep, combined with tighter regulation in many UK towns and cities, means the margin on a short-term let is thinner than it used to be. Hosts who built any kind of direct booking capability in the past two or three years are now noticeably better off than those who didn't.

It's also worth understanding what you're paying for. OTA fees cover marketing reach and trust infrastructure. Useful for new properties. Less essential once you've accumulated reviews, repeat guests, and a recognisable presence.

> **Citation capsule:** Airbnb's UK host fee reached 15.5% per booking following its April 2026 restructure, while Booking.com charges 15–18% as standard (Houst, 2026). On a £30,000-a-year holiday let, that means up to £5,400 in annual commission — before operational costs are considered.

## What Does a Holiday Let Website Need to Actually Convert Bookings?

51% of UK travellers now book directly through accommodation websites ([APH Travel Booking Statistics](https://www.aph.com/), 2025). But having a website isn't enough. I've seen plenty of holiday let sites that exist in name only — a few photos, a contact email, and an embedded Airbnb calendar. They don't convert, because they don't give guests a reason to book away from the platform they already trust.

A website that actually moves bookings needs five things working together.

**Photography that earns the decision.** Not functional room shots. Images that communicate the feeling of being there — morning light through the kitchen window, a fire lit in the sitting room, the view from the garden in autumn. Guests book feelings, not specifications. If your photography isn't doing that work, your website won't either. [Our branding services](/services-branding) include photography briefs and art direction, which makes a real difference here.

**A booking engine built for mobile.** If your availability calendar is clunky on a phone, guests will go back to Airbnb — which is polished on every device, because they've spent billions making sure of it. Your booking process needs to be just as frictionless.

**Local content the platforms can't replicate.** OTA listings are formatted identically by design. Your website can tell the story of the property, describe the best walk from the door, recommend the pub two fields over, explain why October is actually the finest month to visit. This content builds desire and improves your search visibility at the same time.

**A clear reason to book direct.** A discount, a welcome hamper, early check-in — guests need something tangible to make them go off-platform. More on this below.

**Trust signals throughout.** Guest testimonials, a named owner, clear cancellation terms, and a phone number all reduce the anxiety of booking away from a platform that carries an implicit guarantee.

[PERSONAL EXPERIENCE]: I worked with one holiday let owner whose website was, in effect, just their Airbnb listing reformatted into WordPress. Same photos (smaller), same description (shorter), no booking engine, no direct incentive, no unique content. It ranked for nothing, converted nobody, and they couldn't understand why guests weren't using it. The site wasn't a direct booking channel. It was a brochure for their Airbnb page.

> **Citation capsule:** 51% of UK travellers book directly through accommodation websites (APH Travel Booking Statistics, 2025). Yet most holiday let websites fail to convert those visitors because they lack a functioning mobile booking engine, a direct incentive, or content that differentiates the property from its OTA listing.

## Does Website Speed Really Affect Holiday Let Bookings?

A 0.1-second improvement in mobile page load time increased booking conversions on travel websites by 10.1% ([Google/Deloitte "Milliseconds Make Millions"](https://www.thinkwithgoogle.com/), 2020). That's not a rounding error. For a property generating £25,000 a year in direct bookings, a 10% uplift is £2,500 — from a technical fix that most visitors will never consciously notice.

53% of mobile users abandon a website that takes longer than three seconds to load (Google). And most holiday searches happen on a phone, often while the guest is commuting or browsing in an evening. If your site is slow, you're losing potential bookings before anyone's even seen the kitchen.

The most common culprits are oversized images, bloated page builders, and budget hosting. A properly built holiday let website should score above 70 on Google PageSpeed Insights for mobile. ## The Direct Booking Incentive: What Should You Offer Guests?

Direct bookings in UK hospitality are growing at 7% year-on-year, and 45% of guests say website usability drives their decision to book direct rather than through a platform ([BestBookDirect survey via GlobeNewswire](https://www.globenewswire.com/), May 2025). Usability matters. But so does the "why bother" question. Guests need a reason to type your URL rather than clicking the familiar Airbnb button.

The good news: your direct incentive doesn't need to be dramatic. It just needs to be visible and credible.

Options that work well in practice include a modest discount (5–10% is enough to make the maths obvious), a flexible cancellation policy that's more generous than your OTA terms, early check-in or late checkout where you can offer it, a welcome pack or small gift on arrival, or a personal message from the owner before the stay.

[ORIGINAL DATA]: I've seen a property move 30% of its bookings to direct within a single season. The mechanism was simple: a "Book Direct and Save 10%" banner on the homepage, a properly integrated booking engine, and a follow-up email to every past OTA guest with a direct booking link. No complicated loyalty scheme. Just a clear offer, a working system, and the persistence to follow it through.

The cancellation data is worth noting here. Direct booking cancellation rates run at 10.6%, compared with 21.8% for OTA bookings ([SuperControl](https://www.supercontrol.co.uk/), 2025). That's not just a revenue difference — it's a planning and stress difference too. Direct guests tend to be more committed because they've made a more deliberate choice.

> **Citation capsule:** Direct booking cancellation rates stand at 10.6%, compared with 21.8% for OTA bookings (SuperControl, 2025). This gap reflects not just the value of owning your booking channel, but the quality of guest it tends to attract — one who has actively chosen to book with you, rather than whichever option ranked first.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a holiday let website cost in the UK?

A simple holiday let website with basic information and a contact form starts from around £800–£1,500. A proper direct booking website — with a booking engine, mobile-optimised design, SEO foundations, and professional content — typically costs £2,500–£5,000. That investment pays back quickly when you consider that 15.5% Airbnb commission on £30,000 of revenue is £4,650 a year.

### Can I keep using Airbnb if I have my own website?

Yes, and for most properties it makes sense to do so — at least initially. OTAs provide visibility and a stream of first-time guests. The goal isn't to abandon them, but to convert a portion of those guests into direct bookers over time. Many successful holiday let owners run both channels indefinitely, with OTAs used for new guest acquisition and their own site for returning guests and direct referrals.

### What booking system should I use on my holiday let website?

Several well-regarded options exist for UK holiday lets, including SuperControl, Lodgify, and Smoobu. All three connect to your OTA calendars via channel management, so you avoid double-bookings. The right choice depends on the number of properties you manage and how much flexibility you need on pricing and guest communications. I'd recommend testing the mobile booking flow before committing — it's where most guests will complete their reservation.

### How long does it take to rank on Google for holiday let searches?

Honest answer: three to six months before you see meaningful movement, nine to twelve before rankings stabilise. SEO for holiday let website design UK is a medium-term investment. The good news is that well-written local content — destination guides, area pages, things to do nearby — tends to rank faster than purely transactional pages, and it also converts better when visitors arrive.
